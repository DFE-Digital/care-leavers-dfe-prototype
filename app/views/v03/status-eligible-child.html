What is a eligible child’?

Your status is eligible child if:

  • have left care
  • are aged 18 to 25
  • were in care for more than 13 weeks between 14 and 16

Before you became 18, your status may have been ‘eligible child’ or ‘relevant child’.

If you’re not sure about any details, you can ask your local council for access to your care records.

Support you have the right to

Your council must give you the support listed.

General support

Local Offer for leaving care

Your local council’s own support package, which covers things like housing and money

Personal adviser (PA)

Someone who supports and prepares you as you leave care

Pathway Plan

A plan for your future that you do with your personal adviser

Housing and money

Leaving Care Allowance

Money to set up your next home (also called ‘Setting Up Home Allowance’)

Junior Individual Savings Account (ISA) (Opens in a new tab)

A bank account containing money you can get from your 18th birthday

Education and training

Higher Education Bursary

Money to help you go to university or college

Apprenticeships Care Leaver’s Bursary (Opens in a new tab)

Money to help you if you're doing an apprenticeship

Other support you could get

Government-funded services for care experienced young people.

Care Leaver Covenant (Opens in a new tab)

Opportunities and offers for care leavers, including jobs, apprenticeships and discounts

Help at Hand (Opens in a new tab)

Free support, advice and information service for care leavers and children in care

Civil Service Care Leavers Internship Scheme (Opens in a new tab)

Scheme offering care leavers an 18-month paid internship in UK government
