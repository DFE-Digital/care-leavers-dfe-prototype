Leaving Care Allowance

Money to help you set up your home when you leave care. It’s also called the ‘Care Leaver’s Grant’ and ‘Setting Up Home Allowance’.

What is the Leaving Care Allowance?

When you leave care, you’ll get some money to help you set up your next home.

Leaving Care Allowance is also called:

  • Setting Up Home Allowance
  • Care Leaver’s Grant
  • Care Leaver’s Allowance
  • Leaving Care Grant

What can you use it for?

You can use the money to buy things for your home, like furniture and kitchen appliances. It cannot be used for utility bills or rent, but there is other housing support you could get.

Who is it for?

You have a legal right to access the Leaving Care Allowance (you’re ‘entitled to it’) if you:

  • are between 18 and 25
  • were in care for more than 13 weeks between the age of 14 to 16 (this does not include any time after your 16th birthday)
  • have left care

This also means your care leaver status is ‘former relevant child’.

This allowance is for people in England. If you live in:

  • Scotland - you get the Care Leaver Payment
  • Wales - you get the Basic Income for Care Leavers in Wales

If you’re under 18

If you’re under 18 and are a 'relevant child' or 'eligible child', you’ll be able to access the allowance after your 18th birthday.

Check your care leaver status if you’re not sure what it is.

If you want to leave care when you’re 16, you can access the allowance after your 16th birthday.

How much will I get?

If your 18th birthday is after 1 April 2023, you’ll get up to £3,000. Your council might give you more depending on your situation.

If your 18th birthday was before 1 April 2023 and you haven’t had your allowance yet, you should still get up to £3,000 when you move into your first home.

If you got part of your allowance before 1 April 2023, your council will decide what to do.

Who is my local council?

Use the postcode of where you lived when you went into care to find your council.

How and when will I get it?

Ask your personal adviser (PA) or council for the Leaving Care Allowance after your 18th birthday.

You can access the allowance sooner if you’re planning to leave care before you’re 18.

If you’re in a ‘stay put’ arrangement

If you’re in a ‘Stay Put' arrangement, you can ask for the allowance when you move out or after your 21st birthday.

How it works

Your PA can help you access the allowance.

All councils do things differently. Ask your PA to find out how it works in your area.

You may:

  • need to ask your PA to buy things with the allowance
  • get the allowance in one payment
  • get the allowance in smaller payments at different times

How does it affect other support?

Your council should give you this allowance even if you get benefits or other help with money, like Universal Credit.

Issues with this support

If you have issues with accessing the allowance, talk to your PA or someone else from your council.

It might be that:

  • you did not get it when you became 18
  • you didn’t get the minimum amount (£2,000 if your 18th birthday was before 1 April 2023 or £3,000 if it was after)
  • you were not happy with how much you got
  • you did not agree with how it was spent

If you’re still not happy, you can make a complaint.

Further information

To find out more about Leaving Care Allowance, speak to your PA or council.
