Support you should get

If you came to the UK because it was not safe in your home country, this is called ‘seeking asylum’.

As a young person seeking asylum under 18, your local council (part of the UK Government) should help you with:

  • finding somewhere safe to live
  • money for clothes, food and travel
  • school or college
  • finding your family
  • your health and happiness
  • learning English (like finding a course to learn English)
  • planning your future

It is your legal right to get support, but some of it will depend on your asylum claim (ask to stay in the UK as a refugee).

Read our guide to staying in the UK.

Legal advice

If you need legal advice, you can ask your:

  • support worker
  • social worker
  • or personal advisor (if you have one)

They will be able to help you find someone who can explain your rights or help you with your asylum claim.

You can also call one of the free helplines.

You may be able to get extra legal help too, but you will have to apply for it (fill in forms to ask for it).

Support when you are 18

If you came to the UK before you were 18, you may be able to get support as a ‘care leaver’.

Read about how you might be a care leaver in our guide to staying in the UK.

What care leaver support you get depends on:

  • where you live
  • how long you have been in the UK
  • if you are in school, college or university
  • your asylum claim

You might hear about ‘care leaver status’. This is how the UK government works out what support you can get.

To find out your care leaver status, ask your social worker or personal advisor.
