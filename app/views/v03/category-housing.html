Support you may have the right to

When you leave care, your local council may have a duty to help you find somewhere safe and suitable to live (suitable accommodation).

If you leave care at 16 or 17, your council must pay for your housing costs, like rent, bills and other expenses.

If you’re between 15 and 25, you may also have the right to the following support:

Leaving Care Allowance

Financial support to help you set up your first home.

Advanced Claims for Universal Credit

Help to start a Universal Credit claim early, before leaving care.

Pathway Plan

A plan to set out the support you’ll get as you transition to living independently.

Personal Adviser (PA)

A dedicated person to help guide and support you as a care leaver.

Advocacy Services

Someone to help ensure your voice is heard and support you in making decisions.

Support your council might give you

Every council has a ‘Local Offer for leaving care’, which might include extra housing help like:

  • Council tax discount or exemption – money off your council tax bill or not needing to pay it at all.
  • Acting as a guarantor – your council might act as a guarantor so you can live in private rented property or help with a deposit.
  • Help with living costs – financial assistance for things like a TV licence, broadband, or home and contents insurance.

Talk to your council to find out what’s included in your Local Offer for leaving care.

Benefits and other support

You might also be able to get other housing help from the government, which you can apply for:

Council Tax Reduction on GOV.UK (Opens in a new tab)

A discount on your council tax bill if you have a low income or claim benefits.

Applying for a Discretionary Housing Payment on GOV.UK (Opens in a new tab)

Help with rent and housing, such as a deposit or moving costs.

Housing Benefit on GOV.UK (Opens in a new tab)

Help to pay your rent if you’re out of work, on a low income, or claiming benefits.

Household Support Fund on GOV.UK (Opens in a new tab)

Help with essential costs like your energy and water bills or food.

Warm House Discount Scheme on GOV.UK (Opens in a new tab)

A one-off discount on your electricity or gas bill.

Universal Credit on GOV.UK (Opens in a new tab)

A regular payment if you’re on a low income, out of work, or cannot work.
