Who is entitled to it

You’re eligible for the Care Leaver's Grant if your status is ‘former relevant child’.

You’re a ‘former relevant child’ if you:

  • are between 18 and 25
  • spent more than 13 weeks in care since the age of 14 — this includes time after you turned 16

Being in care means you were:

  • in foster care
  • in residential care or similar
  • in supported lodgings
  • under special guardianship
  • in custody under the age of 18 — for example, you were in a secure children's home, secure training centre or young offender institution
  • an unaccompanied asylum-seeking child

If you’re a ‘relevant child’ or ‘eligible child’, you’ll be entitled to this when you turn 18.

To find out if you're entitled to this support, check your care leaver status.

The Care Leaver's Grant is for people in England. If you’re leaving care and live in Scotland you are entitled to the Care Leaver Payment. If you’re in Wales, you’re entitled to the Basic Income for Care Leavers in Wales.

What is the Care Leaver's Grant?

When you leave care, your local council will give you a one-off payment towards setting up your next home.

You can use this money to purchase essentials such as furniture and kitchen appliances.

Some people call it ‘Care Leavers Allowance’ or ‘Leaving Care Grant’.

Your personal advisor or someone from your local council can help you with buying what you need.

How much you'll get

You should get a minimum of £3,000.

You might get more, but your local council will decide the specific amount based on your individual circumstances.

How and when you'll get it

Ask your personal advisor or someone from your local council to apply for a Care Leaver Grant when you turn 18.

Once the local council agrees on the amount, they'll pay it into your bank account.

You may get the full amount in one go or over a few months. This depends on your personal advisor and local council.

If you're in a 'stay put' arrangement, you can apply at 18. But you may decide to receive the payment when you move out or turn 21.

If you're receiving other support

You’re still entitled to this support:

  • even if you’re already getting other financial support like benefits
  • regardless of your living arrangements

Issues with this support

Speak to your personal advisor or someone from your local council if you feel you are not receiving all the help you need.

You can also make a complaint.

Further information

To find out more about Care Leaver's Grant, speak to your local council.

You may also be entitled to a council tax exemption based on your ‘Local Offer’.
