Who can get this?

Your local council must give you a ‘Local Offer’ if you’re:

  • between 16 and 25
  • spent more than 13 weeks in care since the age of 14 — this includes time after you turned 16

Being in care means you were:

  • in foster care
  • in residential care
  • under special guardianship
  • in custody under the age of 18 — for example, you were in a secure children's home, secure training centre or young offender institution
  • an unaccompanied asylum-seeking child

It does not matter if you have left care or are still in care.

Some councils may also give you a Local Offer if you have been in care for less than 13 weeks as long as you either:

  • left care when you were 16 or 17, and returned to your family home in a planned move for 6 months or more before you turned 18
  • were in care before you were under a Special Guardianship Order

A Special Guardianship Order means a court has told someone other than your parents must care for you.

To find out if you can get this support, you’ll need to check your ‘care leaver status’.

What is a Local Offer?

When you leave care, your local council can help you:

  • find somewhere to live
  • with money
  • to stay healthy - this includes mental health
  • to start or continue education
  • get a job

Every council in England has to provide support for people leaving care.

Some support is available wherever you live but depends on your care leaver status.

There is also support that is specific to the local council that cares for you. This means it might not be available to people in other local councils.

This kind of support is called a ‘Local Offer’.

It’s so you can feel safe and supported as you prepare to leave care and become an independent adult.

Moving around

If you move to a new area that belongs to another local council, your old local council still has to care for you. This means you get everything in their Local Offer.

For example, if you went into care while living in Barnet and moved to Sheffield, you’ll still get Barnet Council's Local Offer.

Housing and accommodation

Your local council must make sure you move to ‘suitable accommodation’ when you leave care. This means somewhere you feel safe and comfortable.

Your council’s Local Offer could include things like:

  • finding you supported lodgings if you're not ready to live on your own
  • discounting your Council Tax bill or making you ‘exempt’ so you do not have to pay it
  • giving you advice so you do not fall behind on rent or bills

If you’re under 21 and were in care when you were 16 or 17, you get ‘priority housing’. This means the council must give you emergency housing to stop you becoming homeless.

For more information about types of housing, read our finding somewhere to live guide.

Money and finances

As someone who has been in care, your council must give you financial support until you’re 25.

Your council’s Local Offer could include things like:

  • paying for your TV license
  • giving you money towards bills
  • giving you money on your birthday or Christmas
  • helping you set up a bank account or access a trust fund

Education and training

As someone who has been in care, your local council must give you financial support if you want to:

  • go to school, college or university
  • work
  • do training

Your council’s Local Offer could include things like:

  • giving you a free bus pass to get to school or college
  • covering travel costs for job interviews
  • giving you money to buy books, laptops and essential clothing
  • making sure you have somewhere to live during the holidays if you’re at university
  • giving care leavers work experience or jobs within the council

Health and wellbeing

Your council must promote and protect your physical and mental health.

They should also support you with living a healthy lifestyle.

Your council’s Local Offer could include things like:

  • giving you free access to leisure centres
  • supporting you to register with a GP
  • helping with transport costs to get to health appointments
  • helping you to arrange childcare, if you have children

Relationships

Your local council must help you to develop and maintain positive social networks.

Strong, supportive relationships are important as you become an independent adult.

Your council’s Local Offer could include:

  • giving you a mentor to guide you
  • helping you to stay in touch with people (like former foster carers)
  • supporting you to re-connect with family if you want to

Other things you can ask for

Your local council also must help you to become active members of society.

Your council’s Local Offer could include:

  • paying for your driver’s license or driving lessons
  • helping you to get a passport
  • helping you to get on the Electoral Register so you can vote
  • paying for things you need for religious reasons

If you're receiving other support

As a care leaver, there is support that’s available whichever local council cares for you. This support depends on your ‘care leaver status’.

To find out what support you can get, you’ll need to check your care leaver status.

Your local council must provide this as well as anything in its Local Offer.

For example, you get £1,200 a year for going to college, known as the 16 to 19 Bursary.

You should also get some money to buy a laptop for college if it’s in your council’s Local Offer.

Issues with this support

Each local council offers different support so it’s important to check what your council offers.

Every local council, by law, has to publish a document that shows what its Local Offer is.

If you’ve moved around a lot and not sure which local council should be supporting you, check who your local council is.

If you didn’t get the support you should have had, you can use our template for getting access to missed support.

You can also make a formal complaint.

Further information

To find out more about the Local Offer, speak to your local council.
