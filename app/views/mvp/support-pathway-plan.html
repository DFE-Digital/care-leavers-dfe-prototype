What is a Pathway Plan?

A pathway plan is your own personal roadmap to help you transition from being in care to living independently. It will help you to feel prepared and have plans ready for when you leave care.

Who gets one?

You have the right to get a pathway plan (you’re entitled to one) if you:

  • are aged 16 to 21
  • have spent at least 13 weeks in care, in total, between the age of 14 to 16 (this needs to include time after your 16th birthday)

It does not matter if:

  • you have left care
  • are in custody
  • you are seeking asylum

This also means your care leaver status is:

  • former relevant child
  • eligible child
  • relevant child

Pathway plans are for people living in England. Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland have similar people and teams that support care leavers.

How does it work?

When you’re preparing to leave care, you will have a needs assessment with your personal adviser (PA) or social worker, to understand what support you need. Your PA will make your pathway plan from what is in your needs assessment.

Your plan will be checked every 6 months to make sure the support is right for you. It will be updated sooner if your care or support needs change.

Before you leave care, you will have a statutory review meeting with your PA and the leaving care team, to make sure you are ready to move into independence.

After you have left care?

Your pathway plan can include:

  • plans for any studying or training you might need
  • support to help you find work (like career advice or work experience)
  • plans to find you somewhere safe to live
  • support for your health and wellbeing
  • help with looking after your finances
  • support for helping you meet new people or things you are interested in
  • details about who supports you in care (personal advisers, social workers and mentors)

Your pathway plan might be different if you are:

  • a young person seeking asylum
  • in custody (like a secure children’s home or young offender’s institute)

Special Considerations

If you’re in custody

Your pathway plan will focus on what support you will need after you have left custody and finding you somewhere safe to live.

If you’re seeking asylum

Your pathway plan will focus on what support you need depending on your immigration status and the outcome of your asylum claims.

Issues with this support

If you have an issue with your pathway plan or you have not been given one yet, talk to your PA, social worker or someone from your council. It might be that:

  • you don’t feel prepared to leave care
  • you have not had enough time with PA or social worker
  • you have not had the right support
