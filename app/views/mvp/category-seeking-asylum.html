Who is this support for?

This support is for unaccompanied asylum-seeking children (UASC). This means you arrived in the UK:

  • when you were 17 or younger
  • alone or without a parent or adult
  • to claim asylum

You might also be called a ‘migrant child’ or a ‘displaced child’.

Social services must take care of you until you become 18.

Support if you are 17 or younger

If you are 17 or younger, social services must help you:

  • find you somewhere safe to live
  • find your family (if you want to)
  • with clothes, food and travel
  • go to school or college
  • with your health and happiness
  • with your asylum claim
  • prepare you for adult life at 18

Support when you are 18

When you are 18, social services will no longer take care of you (you will leave care).

Your local council may give you leaving care support. This support depends on your asylum claim.

Who can help you

To find out more information about support, you can talk to:

  • your personal adviser (PA)
  • your social worker
  • your support worker
  • someone from your local council

You can also call the asylum helplines or contact the Refugee Council (Opens in a new tab).
