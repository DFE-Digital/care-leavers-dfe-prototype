Care terms explained

Learn what the terms ‘in care’, ‘looked after’, and ‘care leaver’ mean.

What does ‘in care’ mean?

If you’re in care, it means you’re a young person who is ‘looked after’ by the local authority. You might live in:

  • a children’s home
  • supported lodgings
  • a young offender institution
  • a secure children’s home
  • a foster home

The local authority has legal responsibility for you and must support you.

Depending on the time you were looked after for, you may have a ‘care leaver status’. Having a status gives you the right to get certain support.

What is foster care?

When you live with a foster carer or foster family. This is also called a foster placement. A foster carer’s role is to look after you as if you were their own child. They make sure you’re:

  • fed and clothed
  • go to school
  • supported emotionally

What is a care leaver?

If you’re a care leaver, it means you:

  • are aged 16 to 25
  • were in a care setting (like a foster home or children’s home) or looked after
  • are getting ready to live independently

This is a big change, but there's help to do this. Learn about what happens when you leave care.

What does ‘looked after’ mean?

You’re ‘looked after’ if the local authority takes care of you for more than 24 hours. This can happen if:

  • the local authority gave you a place to stay (for example, a children’s home or emergency foster home)
  • a court decided that you needed to be looked after
  • a court decided where you must live (for example, in a foster home)
  • you got into trouble with the law and had to stay in a secure place (like a young offender institution or a secure children’s home)

Sometime people may call you:

  • child looked after or ‘CLA’ for short
  • ‘looked after child’ or ‘LAC’ for short

Who is not ‘looked after’?

You are not ‘looked after’ if you:

  • live with a parent or someone with parental responsibility for more than 6 months after the age of 16 (this includes someone that a court has ordered you to live with)
  • have been adopted
  • are under a Special Guardianship Order

What is a Special Guardianship Order?

This is where a judge has given someone other than your birth parents ‘parental responsibility’. This person is called a ‘special guardian’. Your special guardian has legal responsibility to care for you.

What is a care leaver status?

Depending on the time you were looked after for, you may have a ‘care leaver status’. Having a status gives you the right to get certain support.
