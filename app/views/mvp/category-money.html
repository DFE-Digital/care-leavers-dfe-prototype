Care leaver support

Support you might get if you have been in care or ‘looked after’

Help with money

Leaving Care Allowance

Money to set up your next home (also called ‘Setting Up Home Allowance’)

Junior Individual Savings Account (ISA) (Opens in a new tab)

A bank account containing money you can get from your 18th birthday

Higher Education Bursary

Money to help you go to university or college

Apprenticeships Care Leavers’ Bursary (Opens in a new tab)

Money to help you if you're doing an apprenticeship

Managing your money

Personal adviser (PA)

Someone who guides you on how to manage your money and helps you get support

Pathway Plan

A plan you make with your PA that covers how to manage your money and get support you need

Local support

You might get support from your local council’s Local Offer for care leavers

For example, this could include things like:

  • money for your birthday
  • help paying for your driving lessons or driving test
  • money for living costs to cover food, travel and essential clothes

Find out more about Local Offers

Know what support you can get

Use your 'care leaver status' to find out what support you have the right to.

Check your care leaver status

More support

Support that your personal adviser can help you apply for:

Universal Credit (Opens in a new tab)

A payment to help you with living costs if you’re on a low income, out of work or cannot work.

Personal Independence Payment (PIP) (Opens in a new tab)

To help you with living costs if you have a disability, illness or health condition and difficulties with daily living or mobility.

Discretionary Housing Payment (DHP) (Opens in a new tab)

A payment to help you with rent or housing costs.

Council tax support (Opens in a new tab)

Apply to your local council for a reduction on your council tax bill.
