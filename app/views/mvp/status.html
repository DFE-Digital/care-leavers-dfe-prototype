What is a care leaver status?

What it’s used for

Local councils use care leaver statuses to help them understand:

  • what support you should get (entitlements)
  • what your legal rights are

They help make things fair and protect you with the law.

How it's worked out

Your care leaver status is based on:

  • how old you were when you were in care
  • how long you were in care for

Care leaver statuses can feel complicated and confusing.

If you’re struggling to understand what they mean or what yours might be, you can speak to:

  • your social worker
  • your personal advisor
  • an independent advocate

Check your care leaver status

To work out what support you should get, use our service to check your care leaver status.

This service is for you if you’re 15 to 25 and have been:

  • in foster care
  • in residential care
  • under special guardianship
  • in custody under the age of 18 (for example, you were in a secure children's home, secure training centre or young offender institution)
  • came to the UK on your own to be safe (to seek Asylum)

Care leaver status definitions

There are 4 care leaver statuses. These are:

  • former relevant child
  • qualifying care leaver
  • relevant child
  • eligible child

Former relevant child

You’re a ‘former relevant child’ if you:

  • have left care
  • are between 18 and 25
  • were in care for more than 13 weeks between the age of 14 to 16 (this does not include any time after your 16th birthday)

Qualifying care leaver

You’re a ‘qualifying care leaver’ if you:

  • have left care
  • are 16 to 21, or 25 and still in full-time education
  • were in care after the age of 16 for at least 3 months (you can include the time before your 16th birthday)

If you’re under a Special Guardianship Order

Special Guardianship Order means a court has told someone other than your parents must care for you.

You’re also a qualifying child if you’re under 18 and have a Special Guardianship Order in place.

You’re also a qualifying child if you’re 18 or older and:

  • had a Special Guardianship Order when you turned 18
  • were in care before the Special Guardianship Order started

Qualifying care leavers are sometimes called:

  • ‘qualifying child’
  • ‘qualifying young person’
  • ‘person qualifying for advice and assistance’

Relevant child

You’re a ‘relevant child’ if you:

  • have left care
  • are 16 or 17
  • were in care for more than 13 weeks between the age of 14 to 16 (this does not include any time after your 16th birthday)
  • have not lived with a parent (or anyone who has parental responsibility) for more than 6 months

You are still a relevant child even if you were:

  • in custody when you were 16 (for example, you were in a secure children's home, secure training centre or young offender institution)
  • in hospital when you were 16

Eligible child

You’re an ‘eligible child’ if you:

  • are still in care
  • are 16 or 17
  • spent more than 13 weeks in care between the age of 14 to 16 (this does not include any time after your 16th birthday)

If you do not have a status

What this means

If you have been in care for less than 13 weeks, you will not have a care leaver status.

This is because you do not qualify for one (meet the legal requirements).

This does not mean you cannot call yourself a care leaver or a care-experienced person.

Support you can get

Having care experience can make life challenging, so you may still be able to get some support from your local council.

Every local council is different. They will decide what support they can give to you and what you can get from their Local Offer.

You will also still be able to get help from charities and services such as Care Leaver Covenant.
