What is a ‘care leaver status’?

You may have a care leaver status if you:

  • are aged 16 to 25
  • have been looked after by a local authority

Having a care leaver status gives you the right to get certain support. It helps your council understand what support they must give you.

How to work out your status

To understand if you have a status and which one you have, you need to know:

  • how long you were looked after
  • how old you were when this happened
  • whether you’re still being looked after or not

If you need help working this out, you can ask:

  • your social worker
  • your personal adviser (PA)
  • an independent advocate
  • someone else from your council

They can also help you access your records if you’re not sure about certain details.

Former relevant child

Your status is ‘former relevant child’ if you’re aged 18 to 25. When you were 16 or 17, your status was either:

  • relevant child
  • eligible child

You may get support past 25 if you started college or university full time before your 25th birthday.

See what support you have a right to if your status is former relevant child.

Persons qualifying for advice and assistance

Your status is a ‘person qualifying for advice and assistance’ if you’re aged 16 to 20. You were also either:

  • looked after by a local authority before a Special Guardianship Order was made. This order is either still in place or was in place when you were 18
  • looked after at some point between the ages of 16 and 18 but you are not anymore

See what support you have a right to if your status is persons qualifying for advice and assistance.

Eligible child

Your care leaver status is ‘eligible child’ if you:

  • are aged 16 or 17
  • are being looked after by a local authority
  • have been looked after for at least 13 weeks

The 13 weeks must:

  • have started after your 14th birthday
  • include some time after your 16th birthday

See what support you have a right to if your status is eligible child.

Relevant child

Your care leaver status is ‘relevant child’ if you:

  • are aged 16 or 17
  • are no longer being looked after by a local authority
  • were looked after for at least 13 weeks

The 13 weeks must:

  • have started after your 14th birthday
  • include some time after your 16th birthday

Other reasons this could be your status

Your status is relevant child if you were looked after for at least 13 weeks after your 14th birthday and then:

  • went into hospital after your 16th birthday
  • were detained after your 16th birthday (in somewhere like a young offenders institution or secure training centre)

See what support you have a right to if your status is relevant child.
