Higher Education Bursary

Find out what a Higher Education Bursary for care leavers is and how to apply for one.

What is the Higher Education Bursary?

A Higher Education Bursary is money to help you go to university or college that you do not have to pay back.

The Higher Education Bursary for care leavers is sometimes called:

  • the HE bursary
  • the care leavers’ university bursary

It’s different to:

  • the Apprenticeships Care Leavers’ Bursary (opens in a new tab)
  • the 16 to 19 bursary
  • other discretionary bursaries, scholarships and awards

What you can use it for

You can use it on things like:

  • student accommodation or rent
  • household bills
  • books and stationery
  • commuting to and from university or college
  • laptops, tablets, or other tech equipment
  • specialist items for specific courses or disabilities

Who is it for?

You have the right to get the Higher Education Bursary (you’re entitled to it) if you:

  • are between 17 to 25
  • were in care or custody for more than 13 weeks between the age of 14 to 16 (this does not include any time after your 16th birthday)
  • are starting higher education for the first time

This also means your care leaver status is ‘former relevant child’, ‘eligible child’ or ‘relevant child’.

This bursary is for people living in England. Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland provide their own higher education support for care leavers.

How much will I get?

You’ll get £2,000, which you can get in one payment or instalments.

Speak to your personal adviser (PA) or someone from your local council about how you will get your payments.

Who is my local council?

Use the postcode of where you lived when you went into care to find your council.

How and when will I get it?

How do I apply for a bursary?

Your council pays the bursary, so you must apply for it through them.

First, you’ll need to show your council proof you have a place on a course (like an offer letter).

Your personal adviser (PA) can help you to apply. They can also help you make plans to study and find other support available.

How and when it's paid

You’ll only receive the bursary once you have proof that you have been enrolled on the course.

The bursary will then be paid:

  • in one payment or instalments
  • directly into your bank account
  • at the start of the academic term

How and when your bursary is paid may depend on your circumstances if you’re:

  • in custody
  • a young person seeking asylum

How does it affect other support?

Your council should give you this bursary even if you get:

  • benefits or other help with money, like Universal Credit
  • other bursaries or student finance

Issues with this support

If you have issues with getting the bursary, talk to your PA or someone from your council. It might be that:

  • you're having difficulties applying for it
  • you're struggling to get proof of your place on a course
  • your bursary payments are delayed

If you’re still not happy, you can make a complaint.
