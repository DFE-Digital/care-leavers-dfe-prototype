What is a Local Offer?

A Local Offer for care leavers is a support package your council gives you when you leave care. It's unique to each council, so some support and services will vary.

Who is it for?

You have the right to get the Local Offer (you’re entitled to it) if your care leaver status is:

  • eligible child
  • relevant child
  • former relevant child

The Local Offer is for people living in England. Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland have similar support packages for care leavers.

What’s included in it?

A Local Offer must include information around support and services that help you:

  • find somewhere to live
  • pay for living costs
  • find and stay in work, education or training
  • take care of your health and wellbeing
  • develop positive relationships and support networks
  • be an active part of your community

It must cover:

  • support you have the right to (based on your care leaver status)
  • any extra support from your council

Extra support

Each council can offer extra support, which could include things like:

  • help with housing costs, like council tax support
  • mental health support, like counselling or other talking therapies
  • help with travel, like a bus pass or free driving lessons

How and when will I get it?

Your Local Offer comes from the council of the area you lived in when you first went into care. Your personal adviser (PA) must show you the Local Offer before you leave care.

You might get it:

  • as a printed booklet or leaflet
  • on the council’s website
  • in different languages

If you move areas

If you move to a new council, your old council must still give you their Local Offer. Both councils might work together to provide you with the right support in your new area.

Issues with your Local Offer

If you have an issue with your Local Offer, talk to your PA, or someone from your council. It might be that you:

  • were not told about your Local Offer
  • are struggling to get support included in it
  • have moved so are not sure what support you can get

If you’re still not happy, you can make a complaint (Opens in a new tab).

Who is my local council?

Use the postcode of where you lived when you went into care to find your council (opens in a new tab).
