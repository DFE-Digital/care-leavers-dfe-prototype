Care leaver support

Support you might get if you have been in care or ‘looked after’

Help with your health

Personal adviser (PA)

Someone who supports and prepares you as you leave care

Pathway Plan

A plan for your future that you do with your personal adviser

Care Leaver Covenant (Opens in a new tab)

Opportunities and offers for care leavers, including jobs, apprenticeships and discounts

Local support

You might get support from your local council’s Local Offer for care leavers

For example, this could include things like:

  • money for medication, the dentist or eyecare (like glasses or contact lenses)
  • mental health support like counselling
  • free or discounted membership for the gym or other leisure activities

Find out more about Local Offers

You might have the right to support

Local councils use ‘care leaver status’ to work out what support you have the right to.

Check your care leaver status

More support

Support that your personal adviser can help you apply for:

Personal Independence Payment (PIP) (Opens in a new tab)

To help you with living costs if you have a disability, illness or health condition and difficulties with daily living or mobility.

Access to Work (Opens in a new tab)

To help get you into or stay in work if you have a physical or mental health condition or disability.

Disabled students allowance (Opens in a new tab)

Help with extra support costs needed to study because you have a disability, illness or health condition.

Work and health programme (Opens in a new tab)

Help finding and keeping a job if you’re out of work.
