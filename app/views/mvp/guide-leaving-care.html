What happens when you leave care

What to expect as you prepare to leave care to begin independent life.

Who this guide is for

What happens when you leave care depends on:

  • your care leaver status
  • how your local council does things

This guide is for you if your status is:

  • eligible child
  • relevant child
  • former relevant child

What happens and when

Leaving care can feel like a challenging time, but your council's leaving care team must support and prepare you.

Normally, you’ll:

  • start getting ready to leave care at 16 or 17
  • leave care aged 18
  • get support until 21, or 25 if you ask

Meeting your personal adviser

A personal adviser (PA) helps to prepare you for leaving care and keeps supporting you afterwards. They must explain what support you have the right to and help you get access to it.

When you’ll meet them

You might meet your PA when you’re 16 or 17. Your social worker must introduce you to them before you leave care.

They must meet with you at least once every 8 weeks to work on your Pathway Plan.

Checking what support you need

You’ll have a 'needs assessment' to find out what help and services you’ll need when you leave care. This will be added into your Pathway Plan.

Making your Pathway Plan

You’ll make a Pathway Plan to set what support you might need, and your plans and goals for when you leave care.

Your council must review it with you at least once every 6 months. However, you or your Personal Advisor can request a review at any time.

Discussing your future

Before you leave care, you'll have a ‘statutory review meeting’ to chat about your future. You’ll talk about things like:

  • where you’ll live
  • if you want to stay in education or training, or find a job
  • what support you’ll need

Who will be at the meeting?

The meeting must include:

  • you
  • your PA
  • your social worker
  • an independent reviewing officer (they check things are done properly)

It might also include:

  • any carers
  • professionals, like a teacher or doctor
  • an advocate (someone to explain your rights and speak up for you), if you ask for one
  • any other people that support you

Support after you leave care

Your PA must continue to support you until you're 21. They must meet with you at least once every 8 weeks.

If you want them to support you until your 25th birthday, you must ask them to (opt-in).

To find out what other support you could get, view all support.
