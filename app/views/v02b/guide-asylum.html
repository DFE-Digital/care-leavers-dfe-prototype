Coming to the UK

Seeking asylum

If you came to the UK because it was not safe in your home country, this is called ‘seeking asylum’.

You will need to make an asylum claim (get your papers) to stay in the UK as a refugee.

If you are under 18

If you are under 18 and you arrive without a parent or adult, people from Children’s Services will take care of you.

They will take care of you until you are 18 and keep you safe. This is called being ‘in care’.

Children’s services will support you until you are 25 as a ‘care leaver’.

Getting your papers

Making an asylum claim

You will need to make an asylum claim (get your papers) to stay in the UK as a refugee.

Some other words people might use for this are:

  • ‘claiming asylum’
  • ‘applying for asylum’
  • ‘making an asylum application’

The Home Office (part of the UK Government) will make a final decision on whether you can stay.

You can read more about how to claim asylum in the UK or read a longer information booklet for asylum applications.

Who will help you

An asylum claim can take a long time, but you will have people to help you.

This might include:

  • a social worker
  • a support worker
  • a legal adviser (also called a ‘lawyer’ or ‘solicitor’)
  • an immigration adviser (who knows lots about asylum)
  • a translator (who speaks your home language)
  • an advocate (to explain your rights and make sure you feel heard)
  • a personal adviser
  • a foster carer (if you live with a foster family)

If you’re unhappy with a decision

Appealing a decision

If you are not happy with a decision made by the UK Government, you can ask them to look at your files again.

This is called ‘appealing a decision’.

You might get a different result by appealing.

It is your legal right to ask for this if you want to. If you do, you must appeal within 14 days of getting the decision.

What you can appeal

You can appeal an asylum claim decision (about whether you can stay in the UK).

You can also appeal an asylum support decision (about getting money or somewhere to live if you are over 18).

Getting help and advice

Support you will get

As a young person seeking asylum, your local council (part of the UK Government) must help you with:

  • finding somewhere to live
  • money for clothes, food and travel
  • your asylum claim
  • school, college or university
  • finding your family
  • your health and happiness
  • learning English
  • finding training or a job (if you get your papers and become a refugee)

Find out more about support as a young asylum-seeking person.

Helplines

To talk to someone about your asylum claim or your rights, see our helplines for people seeking asylum.

Leaving care

What ‘leaving care’ means

If you arrived in the UK when you were under 18, you will be ‘in care’ (looked after by Children’s Services).

This means when you are 18, you will ‘leave care’.

Leaving care means you will find somewhere to live and start your life as an adult.

More about what happens when you leave care.

Care leaver support

When you leave care, you will get support as a ‘care leaver’.

What support you get depends on:

  • where you live
  • how long you have been in the UK
  • if you are in school or college
  • what's happening with your asylum claim

You might hear about ‘care leaver status’. This is how the UK government works out what support you can get.

To find out your care leaver status, ask your social worker or personal advisor.
