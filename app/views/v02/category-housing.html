Support for leaving care

As someone leaving care, you may have legal rights to extra support.

What you get depends on the time you spent in care and who your local council is.

Advanced claims for Universal Credit

You might be able to apply for Universal Credit early to get in time for leaving care at 18

For the following statuses: Relevant child, Eligible child, Former relevant child, Asylum-seeking person Find out my care leaver status

Setting up home allowance

A one-off payment to help you set up int your next home when you leave care

Available for: Relevant child Eligible child Former relevant child Asylum-seeking person

Priority housing

Your local council might be able to give you priority for getting housing

Available for: Relevant child Eligible child Former relevant child Asylum-seeking person

Council tax discount

Your local council might give you money off your council tax as a care leaver

Available for: Relevant child Eligible child Former relevant child Asylum-seeking person

Advocacy services

Help getting your voice heard and standing up for your housing rights

Available for: Relevant child Eligible child Former relevant child Asylum-seeking person

Asylum and housing support

Your local council must help you find somewhere to live if you’re seeking asylum

Available for: Relevant child Eligible child Former relevant child Asylum-seeking person
